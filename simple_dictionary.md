# Pronunciation Notes

NahVah pronunciation is intentionally phonetic and relatively shallow; words are generally pronounced close to how they are written.

## General Rules

- Vowels are usually pronounced separately by syllable.
- NahVah avoids heavy English-style diphthongs.
- The letter `h` often adds breathiness or soft separation rather than functioning as a strongly pronounced consonant.
- Roots tend to preserve their pronunciation when compounded.
- Some older or highly common words preserve shortened or archaic forms.

## Vowels

| Letter     | IPA  | Approximate Sound   |
|------------|------|----------------------|
| a          | /a/  | clipped "a"          |
| ah         | /a̤/ | breathy/open "ah"    |
| e          | /e/  | say (without glide)  |
| i          | /i/  | machine              |
| o (final)  | /o/  | Spanish no           |
| o (medial) | /ɒ/  | octopus / gosh       |
| u          | /u/  | rule                 |
| y          | /ai/ | eye                  |

## Consonants

| Letter | IPA                 | Approximate Sound                                  |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| ch     | /t͡ʃ/                | chocolate                                            |
| sh     | /ʃ/                 | short                                                 |
| zh     | /ʒ/ or /ʒ̩/          | beige / measure; /ʒ̩/ when standalone conjunction     |
| r      | /ɹ/                 | English-style rhotic R ("rock", "red")                |
| h      | breathiness marker  | lightly breathy                                       |

## Guttural Register

NahVah contains a distinct guttural register that stands apart from its otherwise smooth profile.

| Sound | IPA     | Notes                                                   |
|-------|---------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Urh   | /uʀ̥/   | Back-of-throat voiceless trill; marks evil / damnation    |
| -gr   | /ɢʀ̩̰/  | Stance particle; disgust / anger                          |
| -chh  | /t͡ʃh̤/ | Stance particle; submission / deference                   |

These sounds are intentionally disruptive and avoided in formal or sacred registers.

## Compound Pluralization

In compound words, the pluralizing `-o` is added to the pluralized root rather than the end of the entire compound.

Example:

`UrhBah(o)DiSel`

| Suffix | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| -o     | past |

| -s     | comparative |
| -su    | superlative |

|----------|---------|---------|
